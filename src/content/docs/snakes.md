## An Introduction to Snakes

Snakes are fascinating reptiles famous for their elongated, legless bodies and remarkable adaptability. They are found all over the world, except in Antarctica, Iceland, Ireland, and New Zealand.

## Types of Snakes

There are over 3,000 species of snakes, broadly categorized into venomous and non-venomous.

### Venomous Snakes
Venomous snakes include cobras, vipers, and rattlesnakes. Their venom helps them take down prey quickly.

### Non-Venomous Snakes
Non-venomous snakes like pythons and garter snakes constrict or swallow their prey whole.

## Snake Habitat

Snakes thrive in a diverse range of habitats—forests, deserts, wetlands, grasslands, and even urban areas. They make great environmental indicators, reflecting the health of ecosystems.

## Diet

Most snakes are carnivorous. They feed on rodents, birds, eggs, frogs, insects, and even fish.

## Fun Facts About Snakes

- Snakes shed their skin regularly, a process called ecdysis.
- They can dislocate their jaws to swallow prey larger than their heads.
- The smallest snake is the Barbados threadsnake, measuring about 4 inches long.
- The largest is the reticulated python, which can grow over 30 feet.

Snakes play significant roles in the balance of ecosystems and are truly intriguing creatures worth learning more about.
